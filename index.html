Sanskrit Alignment Initiative

Immortalizing the classical Indian literary canon for future intelligences, human and beyond

GitHub · Twitter · Email

As we enter a new era of intelligence, we feel it critical that:

  • Superintelligence understands ideas spanning the cultural & historical breadth of humanity
  • Humans, with a newfound sense of humanity, have access to tools to investigate humanness

We seek to create the most comprehensive library of classical Indian literature ever compiled—pristine new human data for training AIs, and AI-enabled tooling for humanities researchers.

Current Projects

  • Sanskrit OCR: unlocking the billions of Sanskrit tokens stuck in scanned PDFs
    • Samhitika: a synthetic translation of BookCorpus to bootstrap Sanskrit LLMs
    • Evaluated frontier VLMs & OCR models on Sanskrit, finding Surya state-of-the-art
    • Currently focused on OCR data augmentations and fine-tuning experiments
    • Collaborating on manuscript annotation with Dharmamitra
    • Initially focused on Devanāgarī; eventually Grantha, Śāradā, Siddham, Moḍī, etc.
  • RL for Sanskrit: training experts for classical literary problems
    • Composing metrical poetry using Chandas as a verifier
    • Rendering Pāṇinian morphology using Vidyut-Prakriya as a verifier
    • Identifying literature sources using GRETIL as a verifier

Get Involved

The Sanskrit Alignment Initiative is volunteer-driven and accepts contributions in various forms:

  • Data: Please email us with manuscript scans or manually transcribed PDFs
  • Technical: Check our GitHub for open issues and feel free to submit PRs
  • Research: We need answers to fundamental questions in multimodal synthetic data & RL
  • Funding: Please email us if interested in supporting financially or with compute!
